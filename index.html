About me

Hello, I'm Sadman Hafiz, currently immersed in my Ph.D. journey in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington (UTA). My research interests revolve around Metal Additive Manufacturing, Materials and Microstructures, and Strength of Materials. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Khulna University of Engineering and Technology (KUET), Bangladesh. Throughout my academic voyage, I've encountered and overcome numerous challenges, relying on perseverance and determination. This path has not only enriched me with valuable experiences but has also fueled my passion for design. In addition to my academic pursuits, my professional background has equipped me with a diverse skill set encompassing inspection technologies, reverse engineering, 3D printing, and proficient document presentation.

Beyond the academic realm, I have explored various corners of my home country and nurtured a strong desire to travel the world. Photography, cycling, and traveling are among my cherished hobbies. My extensive collection of photographs serves as a constant source of inspiration, motivating me to continually explore and evolve in the realm of photography. I'm enthusiastic about the opportunities that lie ahead and look forward to furthering my contributions to the fields of Metal Additive Manufacturing, Materials, and Microstructures, as well as Strength of Materials.

Academia

A PhD student at The University of Texas at Arlington

Immersed in groundbreaking research focused on advancing Metal Additive Manufacturing. My work revolves around developing innovative in-situ techniques and optimizing process parameters for the fabrication of Titanium Alloy Ti_Al6_V4. My research not only promises to revolutionize the metal additive manufacturing landscape but also guarantees a new era of excellence, delivering superior yield from both microstructural development and mechanical response. Join me on this journey to reshape the future of manufacturing with the promise of a titanium revolution.
